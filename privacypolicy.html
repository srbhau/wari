What is a cookie?

A cookie is a small file that a website places on your computer to allow it to retain information about your visit to the website. Your computer will only return information in the cookie to the website that initially placed it on your computer. A cookie should not be retrievable by any website other than the one that originally created it. There are two types of cookies: session and persistent. Session cookies only last as long as your web browser is open. Once you close your browser, the cookie disappears. Persistent cookies store information on your computer for longer periods of time, perhaps permanently. Please note that a cookie is not a malicious virus, Trojan horse or spyware. Other than using a very small amount of disk space, cookies do not alter your computer in any way.
